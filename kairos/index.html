How to use Kairos

What you're looking at

Every number is dealer gamma exposure (GEX) — the dollar hedging obligation market makers carry at each strike. Positive (teal, "Pika") = dealers hedge against the move: dips get bought, rips get sold, price pins. Negative (purple, "Barney") = dealers hedge with the move: moves accelerate. The solid yellow row is the King — the biggest absolute node and the market's primary magnet.

Node taxonomy

  • King — largest |GEX|. Price tends to gravitate to it, especially into expiry.
  • Gatekeepers — next-strongest nodes between you and the King. Expect stalls/deflections there.
  • Zero Gamma Flip — where net dealer gamma crosses zero (computed from the full Black-Scholes re-priced profile, not per-strike sign flips). Above it: pinning regime. Below it: momentum regime.
  • Air pockets (shaded bands on the chart) — near-zero exposure zones. No dealer friction: price moves fast through them.

The chart

Real candles (Tradier). Horizontal lines = current levels, thickness = strength. The dot trails are node history — every refresh stamps a column of dots, so you can literally watch a node build or decay through the session (growth beside an air pocket is the classic acceleration setup). Copy Pine exports today's levels as an indicator for your real TradingView charts.

Workflow (Skylit doctrine, worth keeping)

Chart first, map second. Build your thesis from price action, then use the map to confirm or deny — nodes are context, not entry signals. Full Trinity alignment (SPXW + SPY + QQQ agreeing) = highest conviction; divergence = size down or pass.

Units & honesty

$ per 1-pt move (default) matches Heatseeker's scale; $ per 1% move is the SpotGamma-style convention — same math, different denominator. OI updates once daily pre-market; Greeks/IV (ORATS) refresh ~hourly; quotes are real-time. Signs assume the standard dealer position (long calls / short puts) — the Dealer setting stress-tests that assumption, which is the honest version of Heatseeker's proprietary inference.

Panel stats

Net±1% — summed exposure within 1% of spot (the regime that matters right now). EM — expected move to nearest expiry from ATM IV. Δ15m — King strength change over 15 minutes (velocity).
