TOOL // POSITION SIZE

Position Size

Size from your risk, not your feelings. Entry and stop decide the share count.

Max position
—

Works for shares or contracts: for options/futures, treat entry/stop as the contract price and remember the multiplier (options ×100). Risk amount = account × risk%. Size = risk ÷ per-unit stop distance.

TOOL // EXPECTANCY LAB

Expectancy Lab

The math doesn’t care about your feelings. Find your breakeven and your edge.

Breakeven win rate
—
Expectancy / trade
—

Breakeven win rate = 1 ÷ (1 + R). Expectancy = (win% × reward) − (loss% × risk). A positive expectancy repeated with discipline is the entire game.

TOOL // EXPECTED MOVE

Expected Move

The range the options market is pricing, from implied volatility and time.

±1 standard deviation (≈68%)
—

EM ≈ price × IV × √(days/365). This is a one-standard-deviation band — price finishes outside it roughly a third of the time. It’s a probability cone, not a fence.

TOOL // FUTURES SPEC SHEET

Futures Spec Sheet

The multiplier and tick value behind every point — the numbers you must have before sizing a futures trade.

Your stop distance in ticks × the tick value = your dollar risk. Micro contracts (MES, MNQ, MCL…) are 1/10 the size and exist so you can learn without full-size risk. Specs are standard references — confirm with your broker.

TOOL // EQUITY SIMULATOR

Equity Simulator

Five random runs of the same edge — same odds, different luck. Watch variance before it’s your money.

Each line risks the same % per trade with the same odds — the spread between best and worst run is pure variance. If 1% risk already looks scary, imagine 10%.
