Candlestick Decoder
Realistic visuals. Context is everything. Patterns are just clues.

BULLISH PATTERNS

Bullish Engulfing

Large bullish candle completely swallows the prior bearish candle.

Best at support. Shows strong buyer control. Most reliable when preceded by clear downtrend.

Hammer

Small body near high with long lower wick.

Strong rejection of lower prices. Best after clear downtrend at support. Sets up reversal.

Morning Star

Three-candle reversal: Bearish → Indecisive → Strong Bullish.

One of the most reliable reversal patterns when it appears at major support levels.

Bullish Harami

Small bullish candle fully contained within large bearish candle.

Signals indecision and potential reversal. Less powerful than engulfing but good confirmation.

BEARISH PATTERNS

Bearish Engulfing

Large bearish candle engulfs the previous bullish one.

Sellers seized control. Most powerful near resistance after an uptrend. Strong reversal signal.

Shooting Star

Small body near low with long upper wick.

Buyers pushed high but sellers rejected hard. Watch for confirmation at resistance.

Evening Star

Three-candle reversal: Bullish → Indecisive → Strong Bearish.

Mirror of morning star. Most reliable at major resistance levels after uptrends.

Bearish Harami

Small bearish candle fully contained within large bullish candle.

Indecision after uptrend. Potential reversal or consolidation. Requires confirmation.

INDECISION / NEUTRAL

Doji

Open ≈ Close. Pure indecision.

Represents perfect balance between buyers and sellers. Most meaningful at support/resistance or after extended trends. Context is CRITICAL.

Spinning Top

Small body with wicks on both sides.

Indecision with less extreme balance than doji. Often indicates consolidation or potential reversal.

Remember: No pattern works in isolation. Always combine with market structure, volume, support/resistance levels, and your trading rules. Context is everything.

Chart Patterns
Continuation & reversal patterns that actually work in context.

CONTINUATION PATTERNS

Ascending Triangle

Flat support, declining resistance creating triangle.

Breakout typically occurs at upper resistance. Strong continuation pattern. Volume accelerates into breakout.

Descending Triangle

Flat resistance, declining support creating triangle.

Breakout typically occurs at lower support. Strong continuation downward. Bearish bias.

Bullish Flag

Sharp move up (flagpole) followed by tight consolidation (flag).

Breakout continues the original trend. Very reliable when volume drops during consolidation and spikes on breakout.

Bearish Flag

Sharp move down (flagpole) followed by tight consolidation (flag).

Continuation of the downtrend. Same volume dynamics apply: low during flag, spike on breakout down.

Pennant

Tight symmetrical triangle (smaller than full triangle).

Very strong continuation pattern. Often preceded by massive volume spike, then volume dries up in pennant. Direction of previous move = likely breakout direction.

Symmetrical Triangle

Both resistance and support converge symmetrically.

Neutral bias — can break either direction. Breakout direction often determined by strength of move into apex. High probability pattern.

REVERSAL PATTERNS

Head & Shoulders (Bearish)

Left shoulder, higher head, right shoulder at same level as left.

Strong reversal pattern. Breakout below neckline = bearish target. Most reliable when volume decreases throughout.

Inverse H&S (Bullish)

Upside-down version. Bullish continuation after downtrend.

Breakout above neckline = bullish. Equal power to regular head & shoulders but in opposite direction.

Cup & Handle

Deep U-shaped cup with small handle pullback on right.

Very bullish pattern. Often appears on large timeframes. Handle should stay above 1/3 of cup depth. Higher lows in handle = stronger signal.

Double Top

Two peaks at approximately the same level.

Bearish reversal pattern. Breakdown target = distance from peak to valley, subtracted from valley. Volume should be lower on second peak.

Double Bottom

Two valleys at approximately the same level.

Bullish reversal pattern. Breakout target = distance from valley to peak, added to peak. Volume should be lower on second valley.

Wedge (Rising/Falling)

Both support and resistance angled in same direction (up or down).

Rising wedge = typically bearish breakout. Falling wedge = typically bullish breakout. Very reliable pattern with low risk reward.

Pro Tip: The most profitable traders don't memorize patterns — they understand WHY they work. Support/resistance, volume, and market structure matter more than the pattern name.
