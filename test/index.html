How to use Kairos

What you're looking at

Every number is dealer gamma exposure (GEX) — the dollar hedging obligation market makers carry at each strike. Positive (cool green) = dealers hedge against the move: dips get bought, rips get sold, price pins. Negative (cool red) = dealers hedge with the move: moves accelerate. The solid yellow row is the King — the biggest absolute node and the market's primary magnet.

Node taxonomy

  • King — largest |GEX|. Price tends to gravitate to it, especially into expiry.
  • Call / Put Wall — largest positive node above (cap) and largest negative node below (support), tagged CW / PW.
  • Gatekeepers — next-strongest nodes between you and the King. Expect stalls/deflections there.
  • Zero Gamma Flip — where net dealer gamma crosses zero.
  • Air pockets — near-zero exposure zones. No dealer friction: price moves fast through them.
