How to use Kairos

What you're looking at

Every number is dealer gamma exposure (GEX) — the dollar hedging obligation market makers carry at each strike. Positive (teal, "Pika") = dealers hedge against the move: dips get bought, rips get sold, price pins. Negative (purple, "Barney") = dealers hedge with the move: moves accelerate. The solid yellow row is the King — the biggest absolute node and the market's primary magnet.

Node taxonomy

  • King — largest |GEX|. Price tends to gravitate to it, especially into expiry.
  • Call / Put Wall — largest positive node above (cap) and largest negative node below (support), tagged CW / PW.
  • Gatekeepers — next-strongest nodes between you and the King. Expect stalls/deflections there.
  • Zero Gamma Flip — where net dealer gamma crosses zero (computed from the full Black-Scholes re-priced profile, not per-strike sign flips). Above it: pinning regime. Below it: momentum regime.
  • Air pockets — near-zero exposure zones. No dealer friction: price moves fast through them.

The Δ chips

The small ▲/▼ percentages flag nodes that are building or unwinding. They only appear on nodes worth watching: a strike must be at least 8% of the King's size, and it must have moved at least 8% versus ~12 minutes ago (not the last tick). That filters out the tail noise — far-OTM strikes sit on the thin edge of the gamma curve, so a tiny spot move swings their tiny value by huge percentages, which is signal-free.

The chart

Chart view embeds a live TradingView chart for the focused symbol, with the current structure — King, walls, flip, gatekeepers, air pockets — listed in the rail beside it. Top Movers shows the biggest node changes since the last refresh. Copy Pine exports today's levels as a Pine v5 indicator (line thickness = node strength) you can paste into your own TradingView chart to see the lines drawn on real candles.

Imbalance

Per-strike call vs put pressure — bright bars are today's volume, faint bars are standing open interest. WK Ceiling / Floor are the biggest weekly-expiry call OI above spot (where MMs are paid to cap) and put OI below spot (the weekly floor). Sentiment blends the volume ratio with the premium ratio, so a thousand cheap lottos don't outweigh real size.

Workflow (Skylit doctrine, worth keeping)

Chart first, map second. Build your thesis from price action, then use the map to confirm or deny — nodes are context, not entry signals. Full Triad alignment (SPXW + SPY + QQQ agreeing) = highest conviction; divergence = size down or pass.

Units & honesty

$ per 1-pt move matches Heatseeker's scale. OI updates once daily pre-market; Greeks/IV (ORATS) refresh ~hourly; quotes are real-time. Chains are cached ~90 seconds and gamma is re-priced from live spot on every tick, so the map stays spot-fresh without hammering the API. Signs assume the standard dealer position (long calls / short puts).

Panel stats

Net±1% — summed exposure within 1% of spot (the regime that matters right now). EM — expected move to nearest expiry from ATM IV. Δ15m — King strength change over 15 minutes (velocity).
